Why Eposnow and Wix don't sync natively

Eposnow and Wix are both excellent platforms built for very different purposes. Eposnow is a powerful point-of-sale system designed for physical retail — managing transactions, receipts, and in-store inventory. Wix is a website and eCommerce platform designed for building online shops. Neither company has an incentive to build a deep, real-time inventory bridge to the other.

The result is that retailers running both face a structural gap. Every sale in one system needs to be manually reflected in the other. For shops processing hundreds of transactions a week, this manual overhead is substantial — and the margin for error is constant.

StockClerk fills this gap using the APIs that both platforms expose, managing the sync logic reliably and automatically.

The Guardian agent: why a 15-minute check matters

Even the best real-time sync can occasionally miss an event — perhaps due to a brief API timeout, a network blip, or a manual change made directly inside one of the platforms. This is why the Guardian agent exists as a separate, independent layer.

Rather than relying solely on event-driven updates, Guardian performs a full catalogue comparison every 15 minutes. It queries your Eposnow stock levels directly, queries your Wix product inventory, and compares every item. If the numbers don't match, it corrects them immediately, silently, without requiring any action from you.

This dual approach — event-driven sync plus periodic reconciliation — means that even in edge cases, the maximum time your stock is out of sync is 15 minutes. In practice, for the vast majority of updates, it's under 5 seconds.

Handling online and in-store orders simultaneously

One of the more complex scenarios StockClerk handles is when an online Wix order and an in-store Eposnow sale occur within seconds of each other on the same product. StockClerk uses a transaction queue to process updates in the correct order, preventing race conditions from resulting in a negative stock count or an oversold product. The final inventory figure accurately reflects both transactions.

What happens to Wix-only products?

If you have products listed on Wix that don't exist in Eposnow — perhaps digital downloads or online-only items — StockClerk respects that distinction and doesn't interfere with them. Only products that exist in both systems are subject to sync. You can also mark specific Eposnow products as excluded from Wix sync if needed.

FAQ

Common questions

Does StockClerk work with Wix eCommerce?
Yes. StockClerk integrates directly with Wix eCommerce stores. When a sale is made on Eposnow, the stock update is pushed to your Wix product inventory automatically. The integration uses Wix's official API, so there's no manual configuration of webhooks or custom code required.
What if I sell the last item in-store and a customer has it in their Wix basket?
StockClerk updates your Wix inventory within seconds of the in-store sale. If a customer has the item in their basket but hasn't checked out, Wix will show them the item is unavailable before they can complete the purchase — preventing an oversell.
How does the Guardian agent prevent stock drift?
Every 15 minutes, the Guardian agent independently queries both your Eposnow inventory and your Wix product catalogue, then compares the two. Any discrepancy — even a small one — is automatically corrected to match your Eposnow source of truth.
Can I still update stock manually in Wix?
Yes, though any manual change in Wix will be overwritten at the next Guardian check (within 15 minutes) to match Eposnow. If you need to adjust stock, it's best to do so in Eposnow Back Office so the change propagates cleanly to all channels.
